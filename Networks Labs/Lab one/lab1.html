First Emperor of the Roman Empire

Augustus Caesar , son of Julius Caesar following civil conflict after the death of Julius took control of Rome and proclaimed himself emperor of the new Roman Empire Augustus ruled during a time of peace in Europe referred to as the Pax Romana and paved the way for Roman dominance in the continent for several centuries to come.

Augustus was one of the most influential figures in early European history and middle Roman history emperors after him tried to emulate what he was able to do in his time as emperor and early kings tried to imitate his influence and powerful leadership several centuries after his reign.

  • 63 bcAugustus is born, his father dies early
  • 44 bc Caesar is murdered by the senate, Augustus returns from his studies in Albania and discovers he was adopted by Caesar in his will, making him heir to Julius. A new order is established and Augustus serves as one of the chief rulers of Rome
  • 27 bc Following inner conflicts in the Italian peninsula, the senate awards him the title of "Augustus" starting the Roman Empire
  • 14 ad following several years of rule, Augustus dies, succedded by Tiberius
